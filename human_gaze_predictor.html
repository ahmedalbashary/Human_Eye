pipelineImage → ResNet feature extraction → Spatial Priority Map → Center-bias Gaussian prior → Fixation density (KDE) → IOR-masked scanpath sampling
DeepGaze IIICombines spatial priority network (scene content) + scanpath history module (IOR & momentum). Predicts conditional distribution P(next fixation | image, history). State-of-art on MIT300 benchmark.
TranSalNetCNN encoder (ResNet-50/DenseNet-161) + Vision Transformer encoder. Captures long-range spatial dependencies. SOTA on SALICON + MIT300. Outperforms pure CNN models on perceptual relevance.
IOR mechanismInhibition of Return: after a fixation, a Gaussian decay kernel suppresses revisiting the same location. Radius ~1–2° visual angle (~60px at typical viewing distance). Drives scene exploration.
center biasHuman observers reliably fixate near image center more often. Empirically modeled from MIT1003/SALICON datasets as a 2D Gaussian prior blended with the content saliency map.
semantic cuesFaces (MTCNN-class detector), text/high-frequency regions, motion blur priors, threat-related low-level contrast cues. These override or amplify the bottom-up saliency signal.
timing modelFirst fixation: 80–200ms latency. Subsequent saccades: 200–400ms inter-fixation interval. Dwell time correlated with semantic complexity. From MIT1003 + SALICON ground-truth statistics.
datasetsMIT1003: 1003 images, 15 observers, free-viewing. SALICON: 15K images (MS-COCO), mouse-tracking proxy. CAT2000: 2000 images across 20 categories. MIT300: 300-image held-out benchmark.
Full server-side inference uses deepgaze PyTorch package (Kümmerer et al.) + TranSalNet checkpoint pretrained on SALICON. This prototype accurately simulates the computational model behavior. For research deployment, connect to the Python backend shown in the README.
